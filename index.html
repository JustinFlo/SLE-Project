FAQs

What is TSA?

The Technology Student Association (TSA) is a national organization dedicated to students interested in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM). It provides opportunities for students to compete in STEM-related events, develop leadership skills, and engage in hands-on learning experiences. TSA helps students prepare for future careers in technology and engineering fields.

What Will We Do?

As part of TSA, students participate in various STEM-based competitions, leadership activities, and community service projects. Members work on projects related to engineering, coding, robotics, digital media, and more. TSA also hosts local, state, and national conferences where students showcase their skills, collaborate with peers, and compete in events that challenge their problem-solving and innovation abilities.

What is the NJ TSA Conference?

The New Jersey TSA Conference is a statewide event where TSA members from across New Jersey come together to compete in various STEM-based competitions. The conference features events in fields like robotics, computer-aided design (CAD), video production, and engineering challenges. It serves as a platform for students to demonstrate their technical skills, gain recognition for their work, and qualify for the national TSA conference.
